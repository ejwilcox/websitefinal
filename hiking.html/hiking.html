Section Overview

Over 19 days JSP 1/30 (Los Pepitos) traversed 168 km with 6800m of elevation gain from Tamango to Chile Chico through the wilds of Parque Nacional Patagonia. Navigation skills including map, compass, and GPS use were built whilst following the Siete Lagunas track during the first ration period providing ample opportunity to bathe in the pristine waters of the region. The second ration period saw students takking on daily route planning and management along with the introduction of safe river crossing techniques up to Rio Aviles and into Valle Hermoso. Along the way students learnt what plant and fungi were safe to eaet and the consumption of Calafate, Changle, and Chaura began in earnest. The second re-ration at Lago Verde saw some members of the group summiting an 1818m peak whilst others rested, relaxed, went fishing. The third ration period focused on off trail travel including route finding around a waterfall on Estero La Gloria and challenging mountain pass into Valle Amarillo o Las Vacas. By this point Los Pepitos were functioning impressively and deemed ready to set out an an independent expedition across the dry and windy espansse surrounding Cerro Pico Sur and descending to the final destinatino of Chile Chico.

First to Chile Chico

Journal Entry

Date: 03/19/25

One of the longest, most amazing dats of my life. It started by waking up at 4 am after cowboy camping under the stars. The wind was immediately noticeable, blowing Max's gaitters back to where we cooked yesteraday. We packed up camp and were on foot by 4:40. The moonlight light out path, no headlamps necessary. We followed the mining trail for the first hour, dealing with lot's of wind and peeking at the silhouettes of the mountains around us. Ate our oatmeal under the stars with warm water then pushed on. It was extreme wind the entire time. We kept following the trail until it split in two. We took the wgon way at first and had to backtrack. Potentially a 4 km mistake. The sky began to light up orange and red as we passed the other groups tent. Erez, our designated leader, gave us an inspirational peptalk for a moment, pushing us to be the first ones at Chile Chico. We sped down the mountain, almost getting blown off once or twice. We took a final short break before getting a spectacular view of the valley below us. We pushed until we reached a highway. For the final stretch, we sipped mate and got a picture in front of the Chile Chico sign. Once we made it to town, our attention shifted to finding a milkshake
